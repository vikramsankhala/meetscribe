Urdu · Late Mughal Urdu

Bahadur Shah Zafar

1775-1862 · Delhi-Rangoon exile

Poetic signature

Bahadur Shah Zafar belongs to the Late Mughal Urdu horizon of Urdu literature. The defining concerns here are exile, fading sovereignty, sorrow, remembrance, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

ज़फ़र की कविता राजनीतिक पराजय को गहरे मानवीय दुख में बदल देती है। उनके यहाँ बादशाहत की परछाईं से अधिक निर्वासन की तन्हाई बोलती है।

English interpretation

Zafar’s verse is haunted by loss of home and empire. The poignancy of exile turns his poetry into a document of historical heartbreak.

Why this poet matters

Bahadur Shah Zafar helps readers understand how Urdu poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

Sample 1

A desolate heart

Lagta nahin hai dil mera ujre dayar mein.

English: My heart no longer settles in this ravaged land.

Hindi: उजड़े हुए इस देश में अब मेरा दिल लगता ही नहीं।

Citation: bahadur-shah-zafar-s01 · Diwan-e Zafar · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 2

No one belongs

Na kisi ki ankh ka nur hun, na kisi ke dil ka qarar hun.

English: I am neither the light of anyone's eye nor the peace of anyone's heart.

Hindi: मैं न किसी की आँख का नूर हूँ, न किसी के दिल का क़रार।

Citation: bahadur-shah-zafar-s02 · 1857-era Urdu verse collections · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 3

The misfortune of burial

Kitna hai bad-nasib Zafar dafn ke liye.

English: How unfortunate is Zafar, that even for burial he received so little land.

Hindi: ज़फ़र कितने बदनसीब हैं कि दफ़्न होने के लिए भी दो गज़ ज़मीन न मिली।

Citation: bahadur-shah-zafar-s03 · Diwan-e Zafar · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 4

The captive king

Umr-e daraz mang kar laye the char din.

English: I had asked for a long life and was granted merely four days.

Hindi: लंबी उम्र माँगी थी, पर बदले में बस चार दिन मिले।

Citation: bahadur-shah-zafar-s04 · 1857-era Urdu verse collections · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 5

Passed in longing

Do arzoo mein kat gaye, do intizar mein.

English: Two passed in desire, and two passed in waiting.

Hindi: दो दिन आरज़ू में कट गए, और दो इंतज़ार में।

Sample 6

The ruined dwelling

Baat karni mujhe mushkil kabhi aisi to na thi.

English: It was never so difficult for me to speak as it has become now.

Hindi: मुझसे बात करना कभी इतना मुश्किल न था, जितना अब हो गया है।

Sample 7

Exile

Main woh majnun hun ke zindan mein nigahbanon ko.

English: I am such a mad lover that even in prison the guards feel my unrest.

Hindi: मैं ऐसा दीवाना हूँ कि क़ैद में भी पहरेदार मेरी बेचैनी से परिचित हैं।

Citation: bahadur-shah-zafar-s07 · Diwan-e Zafar · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 8

A fading sovereignty

Ya mujhe afsar-e shahana banaya hota.

English: Either destiny should have truly made me royal, or spared me this bitter irony.

Hindi: या तो तक़दीर मुझे सचमुच शाही गरिमा देती, या यह कड़वी विडंबना न देती।

Sample 9

The broken gathering

Pas-e marg mere mazar par jo diya kisi ne jala diya.

English: After my death, someone lit a lamp upon my grave.

Hindi: मेरी मृत्यु के बाद किसी ने मेरी क़ब्र पर एक दिया जला दिया।

Citation: bahadur-shah-zafar-s09 · Diwan-e Zafar · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 10

The end of empire

Jo chaman khizan se ujarr gaya, main usi ki fasl-e bahar hun.

English: I am the springtime memory of a garden destroyed by autumn.

Hindi: मैं उसी बाग़ की बहार की स्मृति हूँ जिसे पतझड़ ने उजाड़ दिया।
