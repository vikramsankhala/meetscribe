Persian · Safavid

Saeb Tabrizi

1601-1677 · Tabriz-Isfahan

Poetic signature

Saeb Tabrizi belongs to the Safavid horizon of Persian literature. The defining concerns here are fresh conceits, moral reflection, Indian style imagery, wit, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

साएब तबरिज़ी की पहचान नए और अप्रत्याशित बिंबों से है। वे चिंतन और चमत्कार को इस तरह जोड़ते हैं कि ग़ज़ल में ताज़गी बनी रहती है।

English interpretation

Saeb Tabrizi delights in fresh turns of thought. His poetry renews the ghazal through agile imagery and reflective wit.

Why this poet matters

Saeb Tabrizi helps readers understand how Persian poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

Sample 1

Fresh metaphor

Har roz gol-e taza ze fikram damad.

English: Each day a fresh flower blooms from my thought.

Hindi: मेरे विचार से प्रतिदिन एक नया फूल खिल उठता है।

Citation: saeb-tabrizi-s01 · Diwan-e Saeb Tabrizi · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 2

Arrow of meaning

Tir-e maani cho ravan shod, neshan mi juyad.

English: When the arrow of meaning is released, it seeks its own target.

Hindi: अर्थ का तीर छूटे तो स्वयं अपना निशाना खोज लेता है।

Citation: saeb-tabrizi-s02 · Diwan-e Saeb Tabrizi · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 3

Life's brevity

Omr chun barg-e gul az bad-e saba larzan ast.

English: Life trembles like a rose petal in the morning breeze.

Hindi: जीवन सबा की हवा में कांपती गुलाब-पंखुड़ी जैसा है।

Citation: saeb-tabrizi-s03 · Diwan-e Saeb Tabrizi · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 4

The moral glance

Az nigah-e ibarat har cheh bini dars ast.

English: Through the eye of insight, everything becomes a lesson.

Hindi: अर्थ-दृष्टि से देखो तो हर वस्तु एक शिक्षा बन जाती है।

Citation: saeb-tabrizi-s04 · Diwan-e Saeb Tabrizi · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 5

The road and the traveler

Rah ba qadam sakht nist, ba hamat sakht ast.

English: The road is not difficult for the feet, but for resolve.

Hindi: रास्ता कदमों के लिए कठिन नहीं, संकल्प के लिए कठिन है।

Citation: saeb-tabrizi-s05 · Diwan-e Saeb Tabrizi · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 6

The cup of reflection

Jam ham ayina-ye halat-e ma shod.

English: Even the wine cup turned into a mirror of our condition.

Hindi: मदिरा-प्याला भी हमारी दशा का आईना बन गया।

Sample 7

The city of thought

Shahr-e khayal ra dar o divar digar ast.

English: The city of imagination has walls and doors unlike any other.

Hindi: कल्पना के नगर की दीवारें और द्वार बिल्कुल भिन्न होते हैं।

Sample 8

Grace under burden

Sar bar kaf-e dard ham be tabassum ravam.

English: Even carrying pain in my palm, I move with a smile.

Hindi: दर्द को हथेली पर लेकर भी मैं मुस्कान के साथ चलता हूँ।

Sample 9

Subtle satire

Zarf agar tang bavad, darya ham qatra shavad.

English: If the vessel is narrow, even the sea becomes a drop.

Hindi: यदि पात्र संकीर्ण हो, तो समुद्र भी बूंद बन जाता है।

Citation: saeb-tabrizi-s09 · Diwan-e Saeb Tabrizi · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 10

Saeb's manner

Saeb sukhan ra ba tajriba rangin kard.

English: Saeb colors poetry through lived experience.

Hindi: साएब कविता को अनुभव के रंग से भर देते हैं।

Citation: saeb-tabrizi-s10 · Diwan-e Saeb Tabrizi · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition
