Urdu · Colonial Modern Urdu

Akbar Allahabadi

1846-1921 · Allahabad

Poetic signature

Akbar Allahabadi belongs to the Colonial Modern Urdu horizon of Urdu literature. The defining concerns here are satire, colonial modernity, social critique, humor, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

अकबर इलाहाबादी आधुनिकता, औपनिवेशिक बदलाव और सामाजिक पाखंड पर तीखी नज़र रखते हैं। उनका व्यंग्य हँसाते हुए असुविधाजनक सच भी सामने रखता है।

English interpretation

Akbar Allahabadi turns satire into social diagnosis. He captures the absurdities of colonial modernity with wit and bite.

Why this poet matters

Akbar Allahabadi helps readers understand how Urdu poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

The Person

Akbar Allahabadi appears here not only as a literary figure, but as a sharp observer of society who uses wit as a form of critique. Behind the verse is a recognizable human temperament shaped by Colonial Modern Urdu pressures, Allahabad realities, and recurring concerns such as satire, colonial modernity, social critique, humor.

For contemporary generations, this is a poet to relate to as much as to admire: someone negotiating identity, love, loss, power, faith, doubt, and dignity in language that still feels emotionally current.

If they lived today: a late-colonial style satirist reborn, skewering elite imitation, bureaucracy, and fashionable hypocrisies.

Sample 1

Modern education

Hum aisi kul kitabon ka kabhi mafhum kya samjhe.

English: How could we at once grasp the meaning of all these modern books?

Hindi: इन आधुनिक किताबों का अर्थ हम एकदम कैसे समझ पाते।

Citation: akbar-allahabadi-s01 · Kulliyat-e Akbar Allahabadi · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 2

New civilization

Talim jo di jati hai hamein woh bas nayi hai.

English: The education given to us is new, but not always wise.

Hindi: जो शिक्षा हमें दी जा रही है वह नई तो है, पर हमेशा बुद्धिमानी भरी नहीं।

Citation: akbar-allahabadi-s02 · Kulliyat-e Akbar Allahabadi · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 3

Satire on imitation

Hum aah bhi karte hain to ho jate hain badnam.

English: Even our sighs become notorious in this age of scrutiny.

Hindi: हम आह भी भरते हैं तो बदनाम हो जाते हैं।

Colonial manners

Social pretence

Akbar's warning
