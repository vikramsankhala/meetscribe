Urdu · Delhi Classical

Momin Khan Momin

1800-1852 · Delhi

Poetic signature

Momin Khan Momin belongs to the Delhi Classical horizon of Urdu literature. The defining concerns here are romantic subtlety, intimacy, layered address, elegance, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

मोमिन की ग़ज़ल में इश्क़ की महीन अदाएँ और संबोधन की नज़ाकत विशेष रूप से दिखती हैं। वे कम शब्दों में भाव की गहरी परतें खोलते हैं।

English interpretation

Momin excels at intimate refinement. His ghazals reveal how subtle address and emotional tact can produce great lyric intensity.

Why this poet matters

Momin Khan Momin helps readers understand how Urdu poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

The Person

Momin Khan Momin appears here not only as a literary figure, but as a reflective craftsperson who turns lived experience into memorable language. Behind the verse is a recognizable human temperament shaped by Delhi Classical pressures, Delhi realities, and recurring concerns such as romantic subtlety, intimacy, layered address, elegance.

For contemporary generations, this is a poet to relate to as much as to admire: someone negotiating identity, love, loss, power, faith, doubt, and dignity in language that still feels emotionally current.

If they lived today: a thoughtful essayist-poet, helping readers make sense of complexity with grace and precision.

Sample 1

You remember me

Tum mere pas hote ho goya, jab koi dusra nahin hota.

English: It feels as though you are with me whenever no one else is there.

Hindi: जब कोई दूसरा नहीं होता, तब ऐसा लगता है जैसे तुम मेरे पास हो।

Citation: momin-s01 · Diwan-e Momin · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 2

Subtle glance

Woh jo ham mein tum mein qarar tha, tumhein yad ho ke na yad ho.

English: Do you remember that calm which once lived between us?

Hindi: जो सुकून कभी हमारे बीच था, तुम्हें याद हो या न हो।

Citation: momin-s02 · Diwan-e Momin · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 3

Heart's delicate wound

Kisi ka ho ke raha bhi nahin gaya jata.

English: One cannot fully belong to another, yet cannot withdraw either.

Hindi: न पूरी तरह किसी का हुआ जाता है, न उससे अलग रहा जाता है।

Citation: momin-s03 · Diwan-e Momin · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 4

Grace of address

Maktab-e ishq ka Momin hai nirala dastur.

English: The school of love, Momin says, follows a singular discipline.

Hindi: मोहब्बत की पाठशाला का नियम, मोमिन कहते हैं, बिल्कुल निराला है।

Sample 5

Breath and beloved

Us ko dekha to kuchh khayal na raha.

English: Once I saw the beloved, all other thoughts vanished.

Hindi: उसे देखा तो और कोई विचार ही न रहा।

Hindi: वे होंठ ईसा की जीवनदायी साँस से कम नहीं।

Softest pain

Momin yeh ishq bhi kya cheez hai aram nahin.
