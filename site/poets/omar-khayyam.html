Persian · Philosophical Classical

Omar Khayyam

1048-1131 · Nishapur

Poetic signature

Omar Khayyam belongs to the Philosophical Classical horizon of Persian literature. The defining concerns here are time, wine, skepticism, fleeting life, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

उमर ख़य्याम क्षणभंगुर जीवन के सामने एक सजग, प्रश्नाकुल और रसिक मन रखते हैं। उनकी शायरी याद दिलाती है कि नश्वरता के बीच भी अनुभूति, प्रश्न और आनंद अर्थ पैदा करते हैं।

English interpretation

Khayyam’s poetry lingers at the edge of transience. Wine, time, and doubt are not just motifs for pleasure, but ways of measuring human finitude.

Why this poet matters

Omar Khayyam helps readers understand how Persian poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

The Person

Omar Khayyam appears here not only as a literary figure, but as a reflective craftsperson who turns lived experience into memorable language. Behind the verse is a recognizable human temperament shaped by Philosophical Classical pressures, Nishapur realities, and recurring concerns such as time, wine, skepticism, fleeting life.

For contemporary generations, this is a poet to relate to as much as to admire: someone negotiating identity, love, loss, power, faith, doubt, and dignity in language that still feels emotionally current.

If they lived today: a mathematically minded skeptic-philosopher, pairing intellectual rigor with elegant existential quatrains.

Sample 1

A loaf, a flask, a friend

Nani o badah o yari ke ba to bashad o bas.

English: A loaf, some wine, and a companion with you are enough.

Hindi: एक रोटी, थोड़ी मदिरा और साथ में प्रिय जन हो, तो वही पर्याप्त है।

Sample 2

This moment

Hindi: जब तक यह क्षण तुम्हारा है, इसे अनमोल समझो।

The potter's field

The cup asks no creed
