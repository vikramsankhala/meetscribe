Persian · Mystical Classical

Farid ud-Din Attar

c. 1145-1221 · Nishapur

Poetic signature

Farid ud-Din Attar belongs to the Mystical Classical horizon of Persian literature. The defining concerns here are quest, annihilation, birds, divine love, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

अत्तार की दुनिया यात्रा की दुनिया है, जहाँ पक्षियों की खोज आत्मा की खोज बन जाती है। उनकी कविता अहं को पिघलाकर प्रेम और सत्य की कठिन राह दिखाती है।

English interpretation

Attar writes the soul as pilgrimage. His imagery of birds, valleys, and loss maps the difficult transformation from selfhood to surrender.

Why this poet matters

Farid ud-Din Attar helps readers understand how Persian poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.
