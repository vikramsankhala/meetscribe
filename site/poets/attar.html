Persian · Mystical Classical

Farid ud-Din Attar

c. 1145-1221 · Nishapur

Poetic signature

Farid ud-Din Attar belongs to the Mystical Classical horizon of Persian literature. The defining concerns here are quest, annihilation, birds, divine love, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

अत्तार की दुनिया यात्रा की दुनिया है, जहाँ पक्षियों की खोज आत्मा की खोज बन जाती है। उनकी कविता अहं को पिघलाकर प्रेम और सत्य की कठिन राह दिखाती है।

English interpretation

Attar writes the soul as pilgrimage. His imagery of birds, valleys, and loss maps the difficult transformation from selfhood to surrender.

Why this poet matters

Farid ud-Din Attar helps readers understand how Persian poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

Ten public-domain samples

These excerpts are presented in transliterated form for readability across audiences, with parallel English and Hindi renderings.

Sample 1

The conference begins

Ma hama murghan-e rah-e simorghim.

English: We are all birds on the path toward the Simorgh.

Hindi: हम सब सिमुर्ग की राह के पक्षी हैं।

Sample 2

Seek the Beloved

Talab kun an che to ra az to mirobayad.

English: Seek that which will carry you beyond yourself.

Hindi: उसकी तलाश करो जो तुम्हें तुम्हारे अहं से आगे ले जाए।

Sample 3

Valley of quest

Avval qadam talab ast.

English: The first step is longing and quest.

Hindi: पहला कदम खोज और तलब का है।

Sample 4

Annihilation

Chun to nist shodi, hama u mani.

English: When you are no longer yourself, you become entirely His.

Hindi: जब तुम अपने आप में नहीं रहते, तभी पूरी तरह उसी के हो जाते हो।

Sample 5

The moth and flame

Parvana ra ba atish in guftugu bas ast.

English: For the moth, this conversation with flame is enough.

Hindi: परवाने के लिए अग्नि से यह संवाद ही पर्याप्त है।

Sample 6

Beyond reason

Ishq amad o aql ra ba yak su fekand.

English: Love arrived and cast reason aside.

Hindi: इश्क़ आया और बुद्धि को एक ओर कर दिया।

Sample 7

Birds and mirrors

Har murgh dar ayina-ye simorgh khod ra did.

English: Each bird saw itself in the mirror of the Simorgh.

Hindi: हर पक्षी ने सिमुर्ग के आईने में स्वयं को देखा।

Sample 8

Dust of the road

Az gard-e rah-e dust bartar che ast.

English: What is higher than the dust of the Beloved's road?

Hindi: प्रिय की राह की धूल से बढ़कर और क्या हो सकता है।

Sample 9

The secret of love

Asrar-e ishq ra na har kasi danad.

English: Not everyone can know the secret of love.

Hindi: इश्क़ का रहस्य हर कोई नहीं जान सकता।

Sample 10

Journey inward

Rah dur nist, tu duri az khish.

English: The road is not far; you are far from yourself.

Hindi: रास्ता दूर नहीं, तुम स्वयं से दूर हो।
