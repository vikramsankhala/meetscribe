Urdu · Delhi Classical

Sheikh Ibrahim Zauq

1789-1854 · Delhi

Poetic signature

Sheikh Ibrahim Zauq belongs to the Delhi Classical horizon of Urdu literature. The defining concerns here are courtly polish, idiom, discipline, craft, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

ज़ौक़ की पहचान सधे हुए शिल्प और ज़बान की पक्की पकड़ में है। वे उर्दू को दरबारी परिष्कार और बोलचाल की रवानगी दोनों देते हैं।

English interpretation

Zauq represents disciplined craftsmanship in Urdu. His command of idiom and balance of polish with fluency shaped a generation.

Why this poet matters

Sheikh Ibrahim Zauq helps readers understand how Urdu poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

Ten public-domain samples

Primary source trail

  • Diwan-e Zauq

Sample 1

Who lives?

La'i hayat aaye qaza le chali chale.

English: Life was brought to us, and death carried it away.

Hindi: ज़िंदगी लाई गई और मृत्यु उसे साथ ले चली।

Sample 2

Its own will

Apni khushi na aye na apni khushi chale.

English: We neither come by our own choice nor leave by our own choice.

Hindi: न हम अपनी इच्छा से आते हैं, न अपनी इच्छा से जाते हैं।

Sample 3

Human condition

Ab to ghabra ke yeh kehte hain ke mar jayenge.

English: Now in distress we say, perhaps death will be easier.

Hindi: अब घबराकर हम कहते हैं कि शायद मर जाना ही आसान हो।

Sample 4

The decline of old age

Mar ke bhi chain na paya to kidhar jayenge.

English: If peace is not found even in death, where shall one go?

Hindi: यदि मरकर भी चैन न मिला, तो फिर कहाँ जाएँगे।

Sample 5

Language of Delhi

Zauq jo madrasa-e Delhi ke hain to kya gham hai.

English: What sorrow, if one still belongs to the school of Delhi speech?

Hindi: यदि कोई अब भी दिल्ली की भाषा-परंपरा से जुड़ा है, तो उसमें दुःख कैसा।

Sample 6

Measured craft

Sukhan mein safa ho to asar hota hai.

English: When speech is clear and pure, it acquires force.

Hindi: जब कथन निर्मल और स्पष्ट हो, तभी उसमें प्रभाव आता है।

Sample 7

Worldly lesson

Duniya ne tajruba diya to aqal ne sar uthaya.

English: Experience from the world taught wisdom to lift its head.

Hindi: दुनिया के अनुभव ने बुद्धि को सिर उठाना सिखाया।

Sample 8

Courtly restraint

Bazm mein zabt bhi ik fan-e sukhan hota hai.

English: In refined gatherings, restraint itself is an art of speech.

Hindi: सुसंस्कृत महफ़िलों में संयम भी वक्तृत्व की एक कला है।

Sample 9

Everyday realism

Insan ko duniya mein tahammul bhi zaruri hai.

English: In this world, endurance is necessary for human beings.

Hindi: इस दुनिया में मनुष्य के लिए धैर्य बहुत आवश्यक है।

Sample 10

Zauq's maxim

Zauq darbar bhi dekha aur faqiri bhi dekhi.

English: Zauq has seen the court and has seen poverty as well.

Hindi: ज़ौक़ ने दरबार भी देखा है और फ़क़ीरी भी।
