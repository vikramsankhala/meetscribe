Persian · Constitutional Period

Aref Qazvini

1882-1934 · Iran

Poetic signature

Aref Qazvini belongs to the Constitutional Period horizon of Persian literature. The defining concerns here are nation, lament, reform, song, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

आरिफ़ क़ज़विनी की शायरी राष्ट्रीय भाव और परिवर्तन की चाह से भरी है। उनके गीतों में निजी दुख और सामूहिक इतिहास एक-दूसरे से जुड़ जाते हैं।

English interpretation

Aref Qazvini writes at the meeting point of lyric grief and public history. Song becomes a vehicle for reformist feeling and national memory.

Why this poet matters

Aref Qazvini helps readers understand how Persian poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

Ten public-domain samples

These excerpts are presented in transliterated form for readability across audiences, with parallel English and Hindi renderings.

Sample 1

Song of homeland

Ey vatan, ey aziz-e janam.

English: O homeland, beloved of my soul.

Hindi: हे वतन, तू मेरी जान का अज़ीज़ है।

Sample 2

Lament of the age

Dard-e zaman ra be naghma goftam.

English: I voiced the pain of the age through song.

Hindi: मैंने युग के दर्द को गीत में व्यक्त किया।

Sample 3

Constitutional hope

Azadi ra be awaz talab kardam.

English: I raised my voice to call for freedom.

Hindi: मैंने स्वतंत्रता की मांग के लिए अपनी आवाज़ उठाई।

Sample 4

Public grief

Gham-e melli ba gham-e fardi dar amikht.

English: National sorrow merged with personal grief.

Hindi: राष्ट्रीय शोक मेरे निजी दुख से मिल गया।

Sample 5

The people's song

Naghma agar az khalq bar ayad, paidar ast.

English: A song born from the people endures.

Hindi: जो गीत जनता से जन्म ले, वही टिकता है।

Sample 6

Reformist pulse

Islah ra ba sher rah dadem.

English: We gave reform a path through poetry.

Hindi: हमने सुधार को कविता के माध्यम से राह दी।

Sample 7

Memory of martyrs

Yad-e javanmardan ra be tarana zinda kardam.

English: I kept the memory of the brave alive in melody.

Hindi: मैंने वीरों की स्मृति को तराने में जीवित रखा।

Sample 8

Broken lute, living voice

Gar setar shekast, awaz namord.

English: Even if the lute breaks, the voice does not die.

Hindi: साज़ टूट भी जाए, आवाज़ नहीं मरती।

Sample 9

Patriotic tenderness

Mehr-e vatan ra be ashk amikhtam.

English: I mixed love of homeland with tears.

Hindi: मैंने वतन के प्रेम को आँसुओं से मिला दिया।

Sample 10

Aref's refrain

Aref naghma ra ba masuliyat hamrah kard.

English: Aref joined lyric song with public responsibility.

Hindi: आरिफ़ ने गीत को सामाजिक उत्तरदायित्व से जोड़ा।
