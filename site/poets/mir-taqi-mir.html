Urdu · Classical Urdu

Mir Taqi Mir

1723-1810 · Delhi-Lucknow

Poetic signature

Mir Taqi Mir belongs to the Classical Urdu horizon of Urdu literature. The defining concerns here are loss, love, fragility, ruined city, selfhood, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

मीर के यहाँ इश्क़ और उजड़न एक-दूसरे में घुल जाते हैं। उनकी शायरी निजी टूटन को सभ्यतागत दुख की ऊँचाई तक ले जाती है।

English interpretation

Mir Taqi Mir gives Urdu its incomparable vocabulary of ruin and tenderness. In his poetry, love and historical devastation become mutually illuminating.

Why this poet matters

Mir Taqi Mir helps readers understand how Urdu poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

The Person

Mir Taqi Mir appears here not only as a literary figure, but as an emotional cartographer of love, vulnerability, and desire. Behind the verse is a recognizable human temperament shaped by Classical Urdu pressures, Delhi-Lucknow realities, and recurring concerns such as loss, love, fragility, ruined city, selfhood.

For contemporary generations, this is a poet to relate to as much as to admire: someone negotiating identity, love, loss, power, faith, doubt, and dignity in language that still feels emotionally current.

If they lived today: a witness of urban fracture, recording heartbreak, displacement, and memory with disarming softness.

Sample 1

The leaf and branch

Patta patta boota boota hal hamara jane hai.

English: Every leaf and every shrub knows the state of my heart.

Hindi: पत्ता-पत्ता, बूटा-बूटा मेरे दिल का हाल जानता है।

Sample 2

No one asks

Dikhai diye yun ke bekhud kiya, hamein aap se bhi juda kar chale.

English: You appeared in such a way that I lost myself and even became estranged from my own self.

Hindi: तुम यूँ दिखाई दिए कि मैं खुद से भी बेख़बर और जुदा हो गया।

Citation: mir-taqi-mir-s02 · Classical Urdu ghazal anthologies · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 3

Delhi's ruin

Dilli jo ek shahr tha alam mein intikhab.

English: Delhi, once a city chosen above all the world, lies devastated in memory.

Hindi: दिल्ली, जो कभी दुनिया में चुना हुआ शहर थी, अब उजड़ी हुई स्मृति बन गई है।

Citation: mir-taqi-mir-s03 · Kulliyat-e Mir · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Bewildered heart

Ulti ho gayin sab tadbirein kuchh na dawa ne kam kiya.

English: Every remedy turned upside down; no medicine truly worked.

Hindi: सारी तरकीबें उलटी पड़ गईं, कोई दवा काम न आई।

Mad for love

Mir ke din o mazhab ko ab puchte kya ho unne to.

Hindi: अब मीर के दीन-ओ-मज़हब का क्या पूछना, इश्क़ उन्हें इन सीमाओं से परे ले गया है।

A subtle manner

Nazuki us ke lab ki kya kahiye, pankhri ek gulab ki si hai.

English: How can one describe the delicacy of those lips? They are like a rose petal.

Hindi: उन होंठों की नज़ाकत क्या कहूँ, जैसे गुलाब की एक पंखुड़ी हो।

Sample 7

The city of heart

Ibtida-e ishq hai rota hai kya, age age dekhiye hota hai kya.

English: This is only the beginning of love; why weep already? Wait and see what comes ahead.

Hindi: यह तो इश्क़ की शुरुआत है, अभी से क्यों रोते हो; आगे-आगे देखो क्या होता है।

Citation: mir-taqi-mir-s07 · Kulliyat-e Mir · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 8

A lonely traveler

Rah-e dur-e ishq mein rota hai kya.

English: Why lament on the long road of love, when sorrow itself is part of the journey?

Hindi: इश्क़ की लंबी राह में क्यों रोते हो, जब दुख भी उसी सफ़र का हिस्सा है।

My condition

English: I, you, and even Mir became captives of those tresses.

Hindi: मैं, तुम और मीर, सब उसकी ज़ुल्फ़ों के कैदी हो गए।

The heart remains struck

Mir in neem baz ankhon mein sari masti sharab ki si hai.
