Urdu · Late Classical Urdu

Mirza Ghalib

1797-1869 · Delhi

Poetic signature

Mirza Ghalib belongs to the Late Classical Urdu horizon of Urdu literature. The defining concerns here are metaphysical wit, longing, irony, existential depth, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

ग़ालिब की शायरी प्रश्न, व्यंग्य, इश्क़ और फ़लसफ़े की अद्भुत संगति है। वे भावनात्मक अनुभव को बौद्धिक चमक और आत्मचेतना के साथ रखते हैं।

English interpretation

Ghalib makes the ghazal intellectually radiant. His poetry fuses longing, irony, and metaphysical inquiry into inexhaustible lyric thought.

Why this poet matters

Mirza Ghalib helps readers understand how Urdu poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

Sample 1

Thousands of desires

Hazaron khwahishen aisi ke har khwahish pe dam nikle.

English: Thousands of desires, each so intense it could take my breath away.

Hindi: हज़ारों ख़्वाहिशें, और हर ख़्वाहिश ऐसी कि उस पर जान निकल जाए।

Citation: ghalib-s01 · Diwan-e Ghalib (Nuskha-e Hamidiya and public-domain prints) · Diwan-e Ghalib public-domain prints · late 19th-early 20th c. · p. varies by nuskha

Sample 2

Heart, not brick

Dil hi to hai na sang o khisht, dard se bhar na aaye kyun.

English: It is only a heart, not stone or brick, so why should it not fill with pain?

Hindi: यह दिल ही तो है, पत्थर या ईंट नहीं; दर्द से भर क्यों न आए।

Citation: ghalib-s02 · Diwan-e Ghalib (Nuskha-e Hamidiya and public-domain prints) · Diwan-e Ghalib public-domain prints · late 19th-early 20th c. · p. varies by nuskha

Sample 3

It was no use

Ye na thi hamari qismat ke visal-e yar hota.

English: It was never my fate to find union with the beloved.

Hindi: यह मेरी क़िस्मत में नहीं था कि यार से मिलन हो पाता।

Citation: ghalib-s03 · Diwan-e Ghalib (Nuskha-e Hamidiya and public-domain prints) · Diwan-e Ghalib public-domain prints · late 19th-early 20th c. · p. varies by nuskha

Sample 4

Heart asks what

Dil-e nadan tujhe hua kya hai, akhir is dard ki dawa kya hai.

English: O innocent heart, what has happened to you, and what cure can there be for such pain?

Hindi: ऐ नादान दिल, तुझे क्या हो गया है, और इस दर्द की दवा आख़िर क्या है?

Citation: ghalib-s04 · Diwan-e Ghalib (Nuskha-e Hamidiya and public-domain prints) · Diwan-e Ghalib public-domain prints · late 19th-early 20th c. · p. varies by nuskha

Sample 5

The prison of being

Qaid-e hayat o band-e gham asl mein dono ek hain.

English: The prison of life and the bondage of sorrow are, in truth, one and the same.

Hindi: ज़िंदगी की क़ैद और ग़म की बेड़ी, असल में दोनों एक ही हैं।

Citation: ghalib-s05 · Diwan-e Ghalib (Nuskha-e Hamidiya and public-domain prints) · Diwan-e Ghalib public-domain prints · late 19th-early 20th c. · p. varies by nuskha

Sample 6

A world of illusions

Bazicha-e atfal hai duniya mire age.

English: Before me, the world appears like a playground of children.

Hindi: मेरी नज़र में यह दुनिया बच्चों के खेल का मैदान लगती है।

Citation: ghalib-s06 · Diwan-e Ghalib (Nuskha-e Hamidiya and public-domain prints) · Diwan-e Ghalib public-domain prints · late 19th-early 20th c. · p. varies by nuskha

Sample 7

Who is speaking

Na tha kuchh to khuda tha, kuchh na hota to khuda hota.

English: When there was nothing, there was God; had nothing existed, God would still remain.

Hindi: जब कुछ न था तब ख़ुदा था; कुछ भी न होता, तब भी ख़ुदा होता।

Citation: ghalib-s07 · Diwan-e Ghalib (Nuskha-e Hamidiya and public-domain prints) · Diwan-e Ghalib public-domain prints · late 19th-early 20th c. · p. varies by nuskha

Sample 8

The lane of the beloved

Ishq par zor nahin hai ye woh atish Ghalib.

English: Love cannot be forced; it is a fire that lights where it wills.

Hindi: इश्क़ पर ज़ोर नहीं चलता; यह ऐसी आग है जो अपनी मरज़ी से लगती है।

Sample 9

The image in the heart

Bas ke dushwar hai har kam ka asan hona.

English: It is difficult enough for any task to become easy.

Hindi: हर काम का आसान हो जाना अपने आप में बहुत कठिन है।

Sample 10

No remedy but patience

Ranj se khugar hua insan to mit jata hai ranj.

English: When a person becomes accustomed to sorrow, sorrow itself begins to disappear.

Hindi: जब इंसान ग़म का आदी हो जाता है, तो ग़म भी फीका पड़ने लगता है।
