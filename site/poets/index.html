Persian voices

Persian · Early Classical

Rudaki

c. 859-941 · Samanid court, Central Asia

Rudaki's poetics bind elegance to simplicity. His verse turns courtly polish, spring imagery, and worldly balance into a foundational music for later Persian poetry.

Persian · Epic Classical

Ferdowsi

c. 940-1020 · Tus, Greater Khorasan

Ferdowsi makes poetry a vault of civilization. In his epic imagination, heroism, justice, and mortality become instruments for preserving a people’s moral memory.

Persian · Philosophical Classical

Omar Khayyam

1048-1131 · Nishapur

Khayyam’s poetry lingers at the edge of transience. Wine, time, and doubt are not just motifs for pleasure, but ways of measuring human finitude.

Persian · Mystical Classical

Sanai

c. 1080-1131 · Ghazni

Sanai pushes Persian poetry inward. His verse critiques outward vanity and turns poetry into an ethical and mystical summons.

Persian · Mystical Classical

Farid ud-Din Attar

c. 1145-1221 · Nishapur

Attar writes the soul as pilgrimage. His imagery of birds, valleys, and loss maps the difficult transformation from selfhood to surrender.

Persian · Romantic Classical

Nizami Ganjavi

c. 1141-1209 · Ganja

Nizami refines romance into moral art. His narratives balance sensual beauty with reflective intelligence and courtly discipline.

Persian · Mystical Classical

Jalal ud-Din Rumi

1207-1273 · Balkh-Konya world

Rumi turns longing into motion. His poetry makes desire, music, and spiritual ecstasy feel like stages of return to a deeper belonging.

Persian · Ethical Classical

Saadi

c. 1210-1292 · Shiraz

Saadi fuses elegance with moral clarity. His poetry distills travel, observation, and humane insight into memorable ethical speech.

Persian · Lyrical Classical

Hafez

c. 1315-1390 · Shiraz

Hafez perfects lyrical ambiguity. The tavern, the beloved, and intoxication become layered signs for pleasure, defiance, and mystical depth.

Persian · Late Classical

Jami

1414-1492 · Herat

Jami’s poetry is learned yet gentle. He gives late classical Persian a harmonizing voice where scholarship and devotion support each other.

Persian · Early Mystical

Baba Tahir

11th century · Hamadan

Baba Tahir brings mysticism close to the speaking heart. Plain diction and tender solitude make his verse intimate and unforgettable.

Persian · Lyrical Classical

Khwaju Kirmani

1290-1352 · Kirman-Shiraz

Khwaju Kirmani’s lyric craft lies in polish and musical grace. He helps bridge courtly refinement and intimate longing in the Persian ghazal.

Persian-Hindavi · Indo-Persian

Amir Khusrau

1253-1325 · Delhi Sultanate

Amir Khusrau embodies cultural confluence. His work braids Persian sophistication with Indic sound, devotion, and playful invention.

Persian · Sabk-e-Hindi

Abdul Qadir Bedil

1644-1720 · Delhi

Bedil is a master of metaphysical complexity. His verse thrives on compressed images, abstraction, and intellectual astonishment.

Persian · Safavid

Saeb Tabrizi

1601-1677 · Tabriz-Isfahan

Saeb Tabrizi delights in fresh turns of thought. His poetry renews the ghazal through agile imagery and reflective wit.

Persian · Safavid-Mughal

Kalim Kashani

1581-1651 · Kashan-India

Kalim Kashani excels in delicate ornament. His verse reveals how migration and refinement can reshape poetic texture.

Persian · Qajar

Qa'ani

1808-1854 · Iran

Qa'ani stands at a threshold. His rhetoric remains classical, yet his poetry senses the social and historical shifts of a changing age.

Persian · Modern Persian

Parvin Etesami

1907-1941 · Iran

Parvin Etesami restores ethical urgency to modern Persian verse. Her poems speak with lucidity, compassion, and a strong civic conscience.

Persian · Constitutional Period

Aref Qazvini

1882-1934 · Iran

Aref Qazvini writes at the meeting point of lyric grief and public history. Song becomes a vehicle for reformist feeling and national memory.

Persian · Modernist Persian

Nima Yushij

1897-1960 · Iran

Nima Yushij breaks Persian poetry open to modernity. Form loosens, landscape deepens, and the poetic self enters a new historical weather.

Persian · Modernist Persian

Forugh Farrokhzad

1934-1967 · Iran

Forugh Farrokhzad transforms confession into artful courage. Her poems make desire, loneliness, and female subjectivity newly resonant in Persian.

Persian · Modernist Persian

Ahmad Shamlu

1925-2000 · Iran

Ahmad Shamlu writes with public amplitude. His free verse enlarges lyric space so that dignity, resistance, and love can coexist.

Persian · Modernist Persian

Sohrab Sepehri

1928-1980 · Iran

Sohrab Sepehri cultivates a poetics of seeing. Nature, stillness, and inward clarity become modes of spiritual attention.

Persian · Contemporary Persian

Simin Behbahani

1927-2014 · Iran

Simin Behbahani renews the ghazal from within. Traditional cadence carries contemporary conscience, gendered experience, and civic courage.

Persian · Contemporary Persian

Mohammad Reza Shafiei Kadkani

1939- · Iran

Shafiei Kadkani joins scholarship to lyric feeling. His work preserves tradition while reactivating it for a modern reader.

Persian · Late Modern Persian

Ali Ahmad Saeedi Sirjani

1931-1994 · Iran

Saeedi Sirjani’s literary voice carries irony, conscience, and dissent. His place in Persian letters reminds us that criticism itself can be poetic.

Persian · Diasporic Contemporary

Granaz Moussavi

1974- · Iran-Australia

Granaz Moussavi writes the emotional cartography of migration. Her contemporary lyric turns displacement into intimacy and new poetic speech.

Urdu-Persian · Philosophical Modern

Muhammad Iqbal

1877-1938 · Sialkot-Lahore

Iqbal’s poetry is visionary and exhortative. The self, history, and spiritual striving become dynamic energies for renewal.

Urdu voices

Urdu · Foundational Urdu

Wali Deccani

1667-1707 · Deccan-Delhi

Wali Deccani gives early Urdu lyric a confident musical body. His rekhta joins local speech to Persianized grace and opens the road for the later ghazal.

Urdu · Early Urdu

Siraj Aurangabadi

1715-1763 · Aurangabad

Siraj Aurangabadi infuses early Urdu poetry with ecstatic inwardness. His lyric world feels both wandering and incandescent.

Urdu · Classical Urdu

Mir Taqi Mir

1723-1810 · Delhi-Lucknow

Mir Taqi Mir gives Urdu its incomparable vocabulary of ruin and tenderness. In his poetry, love and historical devastation become mutually illuminating.

Urdu · Classical Urdu

Mirza Rafi Sauda

1713-1781 · Delhi-Lucknow

Sauda couples rhetorical power with sharp satire. His verse expands Urdu’s range beyond romance into critique and public speech.

Urdu · Classical Urdu

Khwaja Mir Dard

1721-1785 · Delhi

Mir Dard writes pain with contemplative softness. His ghazals turn remembrance and inner restlessness into refined spiritual lyric.

Urdu · Late Classical Urdu

Insha Allah Khan Insha

1756-1817 · Delhi-Lucknow

Insha Allah Khan Insha revels in language itself. Wit, experimentation, and social polish animate his contribution to Urdu lyric culture.

Urdu · Delhi Classical

Momin Khan Momin

1800-1852 · Delhi

Momin excels at intimate refinement. His ghazals reveal how subtle address and emotional tact can produce great lyric intensity.

Urdu · Delhi Classical

Sheikh Ibrahim Zauq

1789-1854 · Delhi

Zauq represents disciplined craftsmanship in Urdu. His command of idiom and balance of polish with fluency shaped a generation.

Urdu · Late Classical Urdu

Mirza Ghalib

1797-1869 · Delhi

Ghalib makes the ghazal intellectually radiant. His poetry fuses longing, irony, and metaphysical inquiry into inexhaustible lyric thought.

Urdu · Late Mughal Urdu

Bahadur Shah Zafar

1775-1862 · Delhi-Rangoon exile

Zafar’s verse is haunted by loss of home and empire. The poignancy of exile turns his poetry into a document of historical heartbreak.

Urdu · Rekhta Refinement

Dagh Dehlvi

1831-1905 · Delhi-Rampur-Hyderabad

Dagh Dehlvi perfects urbane fluency. His ghazals feel conversational yet exquisitely shaped, carrying romance with effortless charm.

Urdu · Colonial Modern Urdu

Akbar Allahabadi

1846-1921 · Allahabad

Akbar Allahabadi turns satire into social diagnosis. He captures the absurdities of colonial modernity with wit and bite.

Urdu · Reformist Urdu

Altaf Hussain Hali

1837-1914 · Panipat

Hali helps reorient Urdu toward reform and realism. He reimagines poetry as a moral and social instrument without abandoning feeling.

Urdu-Persian · Philosophical Modern

Muhammad Iqbal

1877-1938 · Sialkot-Lahore

Iqbal’s poetry is visionary and exhortative. The self, history, and spiritual striving become dynamic energies for renewal.

Urdu · Romantic-Political Modern

Hasrat Mohani

1875-1951 · Mohan-Aligarh

Hasrat Mohani shows that romantic longing and political commitment can inhabit the same lyric self. His voice is tender and defiant at once.

Urdu · Romantic Modern Urdu

Jigar Moradabadi

1890-1960 · Moradabad

Jigar Moradabadi’s poetry sings. Passion, intoxication, and rhythmic warmth make his ghazals resonant and memorable.

Urdu · Modern Urdu

Firaq Gorakhpuri

1896-1982 · Gorakhpur-Allahabad

Firaq expands Urdu lyric with sensuous detail and human warmth. His work often carries the climate, memory, and textures of the subcontinent.

Urdu · Revolutionary Modern Urdu

Josh Malihabadi

1898-1982 · Malihabad

Josh Malihabadi writes with thunderous rhetorical force. His poetry is charged with rebellion, public energy, and emotional excess.

Urdu · Progressive Urdu

Faiz Ahmad Faiz

1911-1984 · Sialkot-Lahore

Faiz joins the intimate and the historical with unmatched grace. Love, loss, and collective longing flow together in a language of hope.

Urdu · Modernist Urdu

Noon Meem Rashid

1910-1975 · Punjab-Iran

Noon Meem Rashid is central to Urdu modernism. His experiments in free verse open new spaces for alienation, thought, and modern subjectivity.

Urdu · Modernist Urdu

Miraji

1912-1949 · Punjab

Miraji brings psychological and symbolic experimentation to Urdu. Desire becomes strange, layered, and formally exploratory in his poems.

Urdu · Progressive Urdu

Kaifi Azmi

1919-2002 · Azamgarh-Mumbai

Kaifi Azmi’s poetry links commitment with tenderness. His public conscience never loses the warmth of intimate lyric feeling.

Urdu · Progressive Urdu

Sahir Ludhianvi

1921-1980 · Ludhiana-Mumbai

Sahir makes lyric song socially alert. His poetry is memorable not only for romance, but for its refusal to sentimentalize injustice.

Urdu · Progressive Popular Urdu

Majrooh Sultanpuri

1919-2000 · Sultanpur-Mumbai

Majrooh Sultanpuri proves that elegance can remain popular. His poetry moves easily between romance, song, and resistance.

Urdu · Post-Partition Urdu

Nasir Kazmi

1925-1972 · Ambala-Lahore

Nasir Kazmi writes melancholy with extraordinary delicacy. Rain, memory, and post-Partition displacement settle into a quiet persistent ache.

Urdu · Contemporary Urdu

Ahmad Faraz writes with emotional directness and civic unease. His popularity rests on clarity, lyric charm, and moral resonance.

Urdu · Contemporary Urdu

Parveen Shakir refreshes Urdu lyric with feminine self-awareness. Fragility, fragrance, and modern emotional nuance shape her distinct voice.

Urdu · Late Modern Urdu

Jaun Elia’s poetry thrives on estrangement and irony. His voice is intensely personal, intellectually restless, and memorably self-wounding.

Urdu · Contemporary Urdu

Nida Fazli practices lucid humanism. His poems turn urban solitude and ordinary relationships into meditative lyric moments.
