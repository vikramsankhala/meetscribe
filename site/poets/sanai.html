Persian · Mystical Classical

Sanai

c. 1080-1131 · Ghazni

Poetic signature

Sanai belongs to the Mystical Classical horizon of Persian literature. The defining concerns here are ethics, inward reform, spiritual satire, mystical awakening, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

सनाई की आवाज़ मनुष्य को भीतर से बदलने की पुकार है। वे बाहरी आडंबर पर व्यंग्य करते हुए आत्मशुद्धि और आध्यात्मिक सजगता को कविता का केंद्र बनाते हैं।

English interpretation

Sanai pushes Persian poetry inward. His verse critiques outward vanity and turns poetry into an ethical and mystical summons.

Why this poet matters

Sanai helps readers understand how Persian poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

Sample 1

Wake from heedlessness

Az khab-e ghaflat bar ay ey dust.

English: Rise, friend, from the sleep of heedlessness.

Hindi: मित्र, ग़फ़लत की नींद से जागो।

Citation: sanai-s01 · Hadiqat al-Haqiqa · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 2

The heart as Kaaba

Dil kaba-ye ishq ast, be har su naraw.

English: The heart is the Kaaba of love; do not wander carelessly away from it.

Hindi: दिल इश्क़ का काबा है; यूँ ही हर दिशा में भटक मत जाओ।

Citation: sanai-s02 · Public-domain Sufi Persian anthologies · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 3

Outward piety mocked

Zahid be libas ast, na be nur-e yaqin.

English: The ascetic may have robes, but not always the light of certitude.

Hindi: ज़ाहिद के पास वेशभूषा तो हो सकती है, पर यक़ीन की रोशनी आवश्यक नहीं।

Citation: sanai-s03 · Hadiqat al-Haqiqa · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 4

Truth before display

Haqiqat talab, na nam o nishan.

English: Seek truth, not reputation and signs of status.

Hindi: सत्य की खोज करो, नाम और प्रतिष्ठा की नहीं।

Citation: sanai-s04 · Public-domain Sufi Persian anthologies · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 5

Inner reform

Har ke khod ra na shenakht, khoda ra na shenakht.

English: Whoever has not known the self has not known God.

Hindi: जिसने स्वयं को नहीं जाना, उसने ईश्वर को भी नहीं जाना।

Citation: sanai-s05 · Hadiqat al-Haqiqa · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 6

Worldly illusion

In bazicha-ye khak dar nazar mabin.

English: Do not mistake this dusty game for lasting reality.

Hindi: इस धूल भरे खेल को स्थायी सत्य मत समझो।

The path is difficult

Rah-e ishq ra sar o jaan bayad.

English: The path of love demands the whole head and soul.

Hindi: इश्क़ की राह सिर और जान दोनों मांगती है।

Humility of the seeker

English: To lay one's head in humility on the path of the guide is true courtesy.

Hindi: मार्गदर्शक की राह में विनम्रता से सिर झुकाना ही सच्चा अदब है।

Sample 9

The mirror of conscience

English: Clean the rust from the mirror of the heart.

Hindi: दिल के आईने से जंग साफ़ करो।

Sample 10

The wise warning

Har nafas rahzan ast gar hosh nist.

English: Every breath can become a thief if awareness is absent.

Hindi: यदि सजगता न हो, तो हर सांस भी लुटेरा बन सकती है।
