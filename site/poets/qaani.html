Persian · Qajar

Qa'ani

1808-1854 · Iran

Poetic signature

Qa'ani belongs to the Qajar horizon of Persian literature. The defining concerns here are rhetoric, satire, praise, transition to modernity, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

क़ाआनी शास्त्रीय फ़ारसी शैली को आधुनिक संक्रमण के क्षण में जीते हैं। उनके यहाँ आलंकारिक शक्ति और सामाजिक दृष्टि दोनों मिलती हैं।

English interpretation

Qa'ani stands at a threshold. His rhetoric remains classical, yet his poetry senses the social and historical shifts of a changing age.

Why this poet matters

Qa'ani helps readers understand how Persian poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

Sample 1

Rhetoric and mirror

Fasahat agar ayina shavad, haq namayan ast.

English: When eloquence becomes a mirror, truth appears clearly.

Hindi: जब वाक्पटुता आईना बनती है, तो सत्य स्पष्ट दिखाई देता है।

Citation: qaani-s01 · Diwan-e Qa'ani · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 2

Voice of transition

Daur digar shod o sukhan ham digar shod.

English: The age changed, and poetry changed with it.

Hindi: युग बदला, और कविता भी उसके साथ बदल गई।

Citation: qaani-s02 · Diwan-e Qa'ani · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 3

Satirical edge

Tabassum ra sipar kardam baray-e naqdi zahan.

English: I used a smile as a shield for sharp critique.

Hindi: मैंने तीखी आलोचना के लिए मुस्कान को ढाल बनाया।

Citation: qaani-s03 · Diwan-e Qa'ani · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 4

The speaking city

Dar bazm-e shahr har sokhan siyasat ast.

English: In the city's gathering, every utterance is political.

Hindi: शहर की महफ़िल में हर कथन राजनीति से जुड़ जाता है।

Citation: qaani-s04 · Diwan-e Qa'ani · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 5

Classical pulse

Rag-e kohan dar tan-e tajdid mi zened.

English: An old pulse beats within a modern body.

Hindi: आधुनिक शरीर में भी परंपरा की पुरानी नाड़ी धड़कती है।

Citation: qaani-s05 · Diwan-e Qa'ani · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 6

Measured praise

Madh be andaza buvad, warna ghulu ast.

English: Praise must stay measured, else it turns to excess.

Hindi: प्रशंसा सीमित रहे, वरना अतिशयोक्ति बन जाती है।

The changing court

Qaani's cadence
