Urdu · Classical Urdu

Khwaja Mir Dard

1721-1785 · Delhi

Poetic signature

Khwaja Mir Dard belongs to the Classical Urdu horizon of Urdu literature. The defining concerns here are sufi inwardness, pain, remembrance, delicacy, giving the poet a durable place within the long afterlife of sher o shayari.

Hindi interpretation

मीर दर्द की कविता आत्मा की धीमी जलन और सूफ़ियाना याद की कविता है। उनका लहजा कोमल है, पर उसकी आध्यात्मिक गूंज गहरी है।

English interpretation

Mir Dard writes pain with contemplative softness. His ghazals turn remembrance and inner restlessness into refined spiritual lyric.

Why this poet matters

Khwaja Mir Dard helps readers understand how Urdu poetry balances image, emotion, philosophy, and historical feeling. This folio page situates the poet within the broader movement from classical craft to modern and global continuities.

The Person

Khwaja Mir Dard appears here not only as a literary figure, but as an inward seeker who turns longing into spiritual insight. Behind the verse is a recognizable human temperament shaped by Classical Urdu pressures, Delhi realities, and recurring concerns such as sufi inwardness, pain, remembrance, delicacy.

For contemporary generations, this is a poet to relate to as much as to admire: someone negotiating identity, love, loss, power, faith, doubt, and dignity in language that still feels emotionally current.

If they lived today: a contemplative guide and spiritual podcaster, translating inner life for an anxious age.

Sample 1

Heart's restlessness

Jag mein a kar idhar udhar dekha, tu hi aya nazar jidhar dekha.

English: Wherever I looked in the world, only You appeared.

Hindi: मैंने जग में जिधर भी देखा, बस तुम ही नज़र आए।

Citation: khwaja-mir-dard-s01 · Diwan-e Dard · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 2

No heart but Yours

Dil hi to hai na sang o khisht nahin, dard se bhar na aaye kyun.

English: The heart is tender, so why should it not overflow with pain?

Hindi: दिल कोमल है, तो वह दर्द से भर क्यों न उठे।

Citation: khwaja-mir-dard-s02 · Diwan-e Dard · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 3

Inward travel

Apne hi dil mein ja ke karo justuju-e yaar.

English: Enter your own heart if you wish to seek the Beloved.

Hindi: यदि प्रियतम को पाना है, तो अपने ही दिल में उतरना होगा।

Citation: khwaja-mir-dard-s03 · Diwan-e Dard · Public-domain compiled edition · various (19th-early 20th c.) · p. varies by edition

Sample 4

The ache of remembrance

Dard dil ke vaste paida kiya insan ko.

English: Human beings were made to know the pain of the heart.

Hindi: मनुष्य को दिल के दर्द का अनुभव करने के लिए बनाया गया है।

The world as dust

Hindi: यह संसार अंततः धूल भरा एक तमाशा निकला।

Mystic fire

The silent zikr

English: Your name I repeated silently within the heart again and again.

Hindi: मैंने तुम्हारा नाम चुपचाप अपने दिल में बार-बार लिया।

The Sufi path

Dard, rah-e ishq mein asani kahan.

English: Dard, where is ease upon the path of love?

Hindi: दर्द, इश्क़ की राह में आसानी कहाँ होती है।
