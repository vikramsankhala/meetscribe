Timeline of transmission

9th-11th centuries

Persian literary foundations gather around courtly polish, epic memory, and early philosophical reflection.

12th-14th centuries

Mystical intensity expands through Sanai, Attar, Rumi, Saadi, and Hafez, while narrative romance achieves classic form.

13th-17th centuries

Indo-Persian circulation deepens, preparing the aesthetics that will nourish Rekhta and later Urdu ghazal traditions.

18th century

Urdu classical lyric flourishes in Delhi and Lucknow through Mir, Sauda, Dard, and the shaping of urban poetic language.

19th century

Ghalib, Zauq, Momin, Dagh, Hali, and Akbar Allahabadi negotiate loss, modernity, reform, and changing empire.

20th century

Iqbal, Faiz, Firaq, Rashid, Miraji, Forugh, Shamlu, and others reimagine lyric form for modern philosophical and political life.

21st century

Digital circulation, performance culture, and diaspora communities carry Urdu and Persian poetics into a new global readership.

Curated as a panoramic literary museum of Urdu and Persian poetics, from foundational voices to digital-era continuities.
