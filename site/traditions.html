Two great traditions in conversation

Persian worlds

From Central Asia and Iran to Anatolia and the Indian subcontinent, Persian poetry shaped elite, mystical, and popular imaginaries alike.

Urdu worlds

Urdu inherits Persian imagery and meters while absorbing local speech, urban affect, and the changing publics of South Asia.

Indo-Persian bridge

Amir Khusrau, Bedil, Iqbal, and many others make visible the cultural bridge between Persian and Urdu literary sensibilities.

Curated as a panoramic literary museum of Urdu and Persian poetics, from foundational voices to digital-era continuities.
