Major themes

Love and longing

The beloved may be human, divine, absent, cruel, or symbolic. Desire remains the central current of lyric experience.

Wine and tavern

Often literal, often allegorical, these images express release, heterodoxy, ecstasy, and a challenge to moral rigidity.

Ruin and memory

Destroyed cities, lost homes, and vanished intimacies give Urdu and Persian poetry a profound historical consciousness.

Ethics and wisdom

Saadi, Hali, Parvin Etesami, and others turn poetry into moral reflection without sacrificing aesthetic pleasure.

Revolt and justice

Faiz, Josh, Shamlu, Kishwar Naheed, and many modern poets carry lyric speech into public dissent.

Nature and stillness

From spring gardens to rain-soaked streets, natural imagery offers both external beauty and inner weather.
